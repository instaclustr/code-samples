Midtown Science Museum officials said a holiday-weekend crowd witnessed an unexpected cascade Saturday afternoon when a floor demonstration robot—wheeled out daily for the “Physics in Motion” show—released roughly two hundred ping-pong balls from an overhead hopper into the adjoining gift shop. The museum stressed that no visitors were injured and that only merchandise displays, a braided floor mat, and a small stack of science kits were displaced. A fire alarm was not triggered; staff described the episode as noisy but lightweight plastic rather than smoke or flame.

Witnesses said the spill began moments after a child pressed the oversized green “demo” button while a volunteer docent had turned to answer a question about the museum’s new planetarium schedule. Security footage reviewed by staff shows the hopper latch engaging in line with the scripted routine; the museum attributes the wider-than-expected scatter to a calibration slip that went unnoticed during the morning sound check, when the robot had been tested on an empty stage without the gift-shop aisle in frame.

Operations director Elena Ruiz said the robot has been taken offline for inspection and that the museum will add a clearer perimeter and a second confirmation step before the hopper can open outside the designated demo zone. The gift shop reopened within the hour after a volunteer team finished sweeping and recounting inventory. The docent involved has been temporarily reassigned to the ticket desk while the education team refreshes onboarding for mechanical exhibits and updates the checklist for traveling floor demos.
